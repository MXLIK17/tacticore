# TactiCore - Simulation Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the philosophy and rules behind the TactiCore football simulation engine.

The purpose of the simulation is to create believable football outcomes while maintaining unpredictability and replayability.

The simulation should feel realistic, but it should always remain fun.

---

# Simulation Philosophy

TactiCore simulations should follow this principle:

> Better teams should usually win, but football should never be completely predictable.

The engine should reward:

- Great players
- Balanced squads
- Strong formations
- Good team chemistry

However, unexpected results should still occur.

Football is unpredictable.

The simulation should reflect that.

---

# Core Simulation Principles

## 1. Ratings Matter

Player quality should have a meaningful impact.

A team containing:

- Lionel Messi
- Cristiano Ronaldo
- Xavi
- Maldini

should generally outperform an average squad.

However, ratings should not guarantee victory.

---

## 2. Team Balance Matters

A squad should not succeed by only having elite attackers.

The simulation should consider:

- Attack
- Midfield
- Defence
- Goalkeeping

A balanced team should compete better than an unbalanced team.

Example:

A team with:

- 95 attack
- 60 midfield
- 55 defence

should have weaknesses.

---

## 3. Formation Matters

Formation should influence performance.

Different formations should create different strengths.

Examples:

4-3-3:

Advantages:

- Wide attacking options
- Strong offensive pressure

Weaknesses:

- Space behind wingers

---

4-2-3-1:

Advantages:

- Midfield control
- Defensive stability

Weaknesses:

- Requires strong attacking midfielder

---

3-5-2:

Advantages:

- Midfield superiority

Weaknesses:

- Defensive width

---

# Match Simulation

Every match should generate:

- Winner
- Final score
- Goals
- Goal scorers
- Assists
- Player performances

The output should create a football story.

---

# Match Factors

The simulation should consider:

## Team Rating

Overall squad strength.

---

## Attack

Influences:

- Chance creation
- Goals scored

Affected by:

- Strikers
- Wingers
- Attacking midfielders

---

## Midfield

Influences:

- Possession
- Control
- Chance creation

Affected by:

- Passing
- Creativity
- Ball control

---

## Defence

Influences:

- Goals conceded
- Defensive stability

Affected by:

- Centre-backs
- Fullbacks
- Defensive midfielders

---

## Goalkeeping

Influences:

- Saves
- Clean sheets

---

## Formation

Influences:

- Tactical advantages
- Weaknesses

---

## Chemistry

Chemistry provides a performance bonus.

It should reward:

- Same club
- Same nationality
- Similar football era
- Tactical compatibility

However:

Chemistry should never make a weaker player better than an elite player.

---

# Home Advantage

Premier League simulations should include home advantage.

A team playing at home should receive a small advantage.

Home advantage should influence:

- Confidence
- Performance
- Probability

It should not guarantee victory.

---

# Upsets

Upsets are important.

Examples:

A lower-rated team defeating a stronger team should occasionally happen.

Factors that can contribute:

- Formation advantage
- Chemistry
- Match randomness
- Defensive performance
- Goalkeeper performance

---

# Goals

Goals should not be generated randomly without context.

Goals should be influenced by:

- Team attack
- Opponent defence
- Player quality
- Match situation

Elite attackers should score more frequently.

---

# Player Statistics

Statistics should accumulate throughout competitions.

Track:

## Players

- Goals

- Performances

---

## Teams

- Wins
- Draws

- Goals conceded
- Points

Ratings represent a specific historical period.

Examples:

Different players:

Messi 2009

Messi 2015

Messi 2022


Each version should have different strengths.

---

# Rating Categories

Players may contain:

- Overall
- Pace
- Shooting
- Passing
- Dribbling
- Defending
- Physical
- Goalkeeping

---

# Simulation Balance

Avoid these extremes:

## Too Random

Example:

A team of legends constantly losing randomly.

---

## Too Predictable

Example:

The highest-rated team always wins.

---

The ideal balance:

Strong teams usually succeed.

Unexpected moments still happen.

---

# Future Expansion

Future simulation improvements may include:

- Advanced tactics
- Player roles
- Substitutions
- Injuries
- Fatigue
- Advanced chemistry
- Tactical instructions

However:

Version 1 should remain simple and enjoyable.

---

# Final Simulation Principle

The simulation should answer:

> If these players actually played together, what would happen?

The goal is not perfect statistical accuracy.

The goal is believable football stories.
